Readiness rule

Earn the intensity, do not guess it

Use sleep, joint feel, and stress to decide whether today is Push, Build, or Protect.

Build day
Push
Sleep 7+ hr, pain 0–2/10, energy stable. Do full cardio block and full lifting volume.
Build
Sleep 6–7 hr or mild stiffness. Keep all lifts, shorten HIIT by 20% if needed.
Protect
Sleep under 6 hr, pain 4+/10, or heavy life stress. Keep warm-up, do Zone 2 only, and 2 sets per exercise.
Pain rule
0–3/10 keep going. 4–5/10 reduce range/load. 6+/10 stop that movement and swap it.

Fail-safe script for relapse weeks
These are the exact situations that usually trigger rebound regain.
If motivation drops and you want to “start again Monday”▾
Do the minimum effective day: 10-minute warm-up, 15-minute brisk walk, 2 exercises, 2 sets each, and one protein-forward dinner. The identity is protected when the streak is not broken.
If the scale spikes after a family weekend or restaurant meal▾
Do not crash calories. Return to the normal plan immediately, push water and steps, and wait for the next 3 weigh-ins. Water retention is not body fat regain.
If two workouts are missed in one week▾
Activate the emergency week: perform all four sessions in shortened form, remove one indulgence meal, and pre-log dinner every day by noon. Recovery starts with structure, not guilt.

Relapse-proof meal structure

Default meals beat motivation

Breakfast stays fixed, lunch rotates between 2 choices, snacks have 2 emergency options, and dinner rotates between 3 protein-anchored templates.

  • Breakfast default: protein-first and repeatable 5-6 days per week.
  • Lunch default: 2 prepared choices only, both with a visible protein anchor.
  • Snack default: one rescue snack at 4-5 PM before cravings take over.
  • Dinner default: 3 rotating templates so family meals stay controlled, not random.
  • Flex meal: once weekly, protein first, no cheat-day spillover.
Diet break logic

Use relief before burnout forces it

Long cuts are more durable when maintenance is practiced on purpose.

  • After 8-10 hard weeks, run a 10-14 day diet break at estimated maintenance.
  • Protein stays high, meal timing stays the same, and training structure does not change.
  • Maintenance is not a free-for-all: it is simply more carbs and calories around training.
  • Return to the cut when scale and hunger normalize, not when guilt hits.

Progression engine

Reps first, then load, then density

This plan builds with precision, not fatigue gambling.

  • When every prescribed set reaches the top of the rep range twice with pain <=3/10, increase load next week.
  • If reps drop hard or form degrades, keep the same weight and win with cleaner execution.
  • If energy is low for 2 straight sessions, cut total sets by 20% for 1 week before pushing again.
  • HIIT stays capped at 2 sessions per week, 10-14 minutes each.

Deload + overload rules
This is how to progress safely without frying recovery or feeding all-or-nothing thinking.
  • Add reps before load. Only increase weight after all prescribed sets reach the top of the rep range with pain ≤3/10 and clean control.
  • Dumbbells: add the smallest available jump. Barbell: add 5 lb total when bar speed and posture stay solid.
  • If HIIT quality crashes, keep the same load next week and improve breathing recovery instead.
  • Every 4th week is a deload: reduce lifting sets by about 30% and cut HIIT rounds by 25–30%.
  • There is no grinding. This plan uses consistency, not heroics.

Pain-scale logic

She never has to guess whether to push through

  • 0-2/10: proceed as written.
  • 3-4/10: reduce range, tempo down, and cut one set.
  • 5+/10: stop that movement and use the listed swap immediately.
  • Pain that worsens set to set ends the exercise for the day.
Unilateral correction

The weaker side always sets the standard

  • Weaker right side starts first on lower-body single-leg drills.
  • Right calf and ankle get one extra set on Wednesday and Sunday.
  • Tempo on split patterns is 3 sec down, not rushed reps.
  • Symmetry improves by control, not by hiding the imbalance with momentum.

Movement laws

What she should avoid

  • No jump-heavy HIIT on bad knee days.
  • No fast twisting or cutting motions when tired.
  • No behind-the-neck pressing or deep painful shoulder angles.
  • No ego loading on lunges, squats, or hinges.
Green-light cues

What she should feel

  • Foot pressure spread across heel, big toe, and outer foot.
  • Knee follows the second/third toe, not collapsing inward.
  • Shoulder packed down with neutral grip pressing.
  • Core braced before each lower-body rep, exhale on effort.
Coach note: the purpose of the stability work is not rehab for rehab’s sake. It is to make fat-loss training more efficient by giving force a cleaner path through the body.

Adjustment ladder

Only change one variable at a time

  1. First check compliance: workouts, steps, protein, and snack leakage.
  2. If compliance is solid for 14 days and progress is flat, add 10–15 minutes of walking on 3 days per week.
  3. If still flat after another 14 days, remove roughly 15–20 g carbs from rest days only.
  4. Do not stack lower calories and more HIIT at the same time.
Maintenance bridge

What happens at 120 lb

  • Keep 4 training days for at least 8 more weeks.
  • Raise calories slowly by 75–100 per day each week until weight stabilizes.
  • Protein stays high; treats remain planned.
  • Do not celebrate by “eating normal again.” The plan becomes lighter, not absent.
Behavioral operating system
The permanent-fat-loss version of discipline is not willpower. It is architecture.
Identity rule▾
Never ask, “Do I feel like following the plan?” Ask, “What does the woman who stays at 120 do today?”
Environment rule▾
Protein foods are visible and ready. Trigger foods are inconvenient, portioned, or absent. The kitchen is designed for the lean body, not the old habits.
Minimum effective day rule▾
On hard family days, do the smallest version that still counts: warm-up, 15-minute walk, 2 lifts, 1 protein-first dinner. This ends all-or-nothing thinking.
Recovery rule▾
One off-plan meal is noise. Two off-plan days plus skipped workouts is a relapse pattern. The next action must be structured immediately.
