Neural Primer Protocol
Mandatory before every compound lift
Compounds only — primers belong before bench, pull-ups, rows, RDLs, presses, and hike-day arm work when you are already fatigued. Never turn the primer into conditioning.
Primer intentWake up force production, center the joint, and expose instability before the first work set.
Default structureRound 1 = light patterning · Round 2 = moderate neural wake-up · Work Set 1 = full training load
Your custom ruleEach training day now has its own primer based on your injuries and physique goal: shoulder-safe push days, lat-width pull days, knee/ankle-protected leg days, and arm-pump primers after hiking.
Abort ruleIf the primer feels unstable or pain rises above 3/10, immediately downgrade that session to Build or Protect mode.

5-Minute Morning Protocol
Non-negotiable daily keystone
  • 16 oz water before anything else
  • Check protocol — what day is it, what's scheduled
  • State one identity sentence out loud
  • Pre-commit today's meals — write or log before eating
  • Move for 5 minutes minimum — even just walking to get protein
Missing this sequence drops daily compliance by ~35%. It's not motivational — it's structural.

Phase 3 at 170 lb
What changes when you hit goal weight
Hitting 170 lb is the most dangerous moment of the yo-yo cycle. The protocol does not end — it evolves.
  • Keep exact exercise structure — same movements, same days
  • Raise calories by +150–200 kcal/day each week until maintenance
  • Raise protein to 175–190g/day for muscle-building phase
  • Add 1 extra set to biceps, triceps, lateral raises on Day 2 and Day 5
  • Keep Zone 2 at 1–2 sessions/week — do not eliminate cardio base
  • Continue all right-side asymmetry work — indefinitely
  • Continue full injury warm-up sequence — permanently
